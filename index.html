Jan Todd: The Woman Who Redefined Strength

Jan Todd is a legendary figure in the world of strength sports and was once known as the "strongest woman in the world."

In 1977, she became the first woman to lift over 1,000 lbs in a three-lift powerlifting meet and went on to set more than 60 national and world records.

Beyond her athletic accomplishments, she has been a tireless advocate for strength history and education. Alongside her husband, Terry Todd, she co-founded the Stark Center for Physical Culture and Sports at the University of Texas.

Her career helped reshape the cultural narrative around female strength—proving that women not only belong in strength sports but can thrive, lead, and inspire.

Timeline of Achievements

  • 1975: Jan begins competitive powerlifting.
  • 1977: First woman to lift over 1,000 lbs in a three-lift meet.
  • 1979: Featured in Sports Illustrated.
  • 1983: Inducted into the Women's Sports Hall of Fame.
  • 2009: Co-founds the Stark Center for Physical Culture and Sports.
